Windows 10


Microsoft has lifted the curtain much higher on Windows 10, showing off a whole slew of new features that will be coming to PCs and phones later this year.

  • Holograms

    The coolest part of Microsoft's presentation was "Windows Holographic," Microsoft's attempt to immerse its customers into a computerized world using the "Hololens".


  • Cortana

    Microsoft's version of Siri will be part of Windows 10. By using voice commands, Cortana can show your notifications, stock information, sports news, and other functions typical of smartphone voice assistants.


  • Start Menu

    After being removed from Windows 8, Microsoft announced that the Start Menu will make a full comeback in Windows 10. It can be viewed as a full-screen Windows 8-like display of tiles or a more familiar Windows 7-like drawer of apps.


  • One Windows for all your device

    There are currently three different versions of Windows: Windows 8.1, Windows RT for tablets and Windows Phone. Windows RT and Windows Phone are just becoming Windows 10.


  • XBOX

    Microsoft said the new Xbox app for Windows 10 will let gamers stream and play Xbox games on their PCs. They can also play games from the Steam video game network and save and share game-play clips.
